IANA(Internet Assigned Numbers Authority)

The internet Assigned Numbers Authority is a standard organization that Oversees
-IP address distribution 
-Root Zone management
-AS number distribution 
-Media Type

*About INNA*
IANA maintains the global pool of Internet Protocol resources and distribute them to the Regional Internet Registries (RIRs)
APNIC as one of the RIRs receives Internet resources from IANA

**What is the role of the number registry?**
The main purpose of a number registry is to distribute IP and AS number resources in a fair, consistent way that meets the management goals of conservation, aggregation, and registration.


*Address Supporting Organization (ASO)*
The ASO is one of ICANN's three Supporting Organizations (SOS)
The ASO advises the ICANN Board on policy issues relating to the operation, assignment, and management of Internet addresses
ASO have an Address Council (ASO AC) consisting of the 15 members of the NRO Number Council (NRO NC)


*Number Resource Organization(NRO)*
THe Number Resource Organization (NRO) is a coordinating body for the RIRs.

*Reggional Internet Registries(RIRs) are responsible for:
-Managing the distribution and registration of IP and AS number in their respective region.

-Distribution of Internet resources in a fair and consistent manner.

**There are five RIRs operating around the world**


*The Asia Pacific Network Information Centre (APNIC)* was formed in 1993 and operates within the Asia Pacific region comprising of 56 economies.

Formed in 2005, the *African Network Information Centre (AFRINIC)* is responsible for the distribution and management of Internet number resources for Africa and the Indian Ocean region.

*The Latin America and Caribbean Network Information Centre (LACNIC)* was established in 2002. It is responsible for assigning an administrating Internet number resources for the region of Latin America and the Caribbean region.


*American Registry for Internet Numbers (ARIN)* operates in Canada, many Caribbean and North Atlantic islands, and the United States.

*RIR Members*
RIR members are comprised of Internet service providers (ISPs), hosting/data centre, governments, universities, financial institutions, end users, for-profit and not-for-profit enterprises of all sizes and across all business sectors.


National Internet Registries
As the RIR for the Asia Pacific, APNIC provides Internet number resource distribution, registration, and many other services across the region.
In some cases APNIC provides services through approved NIR organizations to meet particular geographical needs. NIRs perform Internet number delegations and registrations in line with APNIC policies.

National Internet Registries
-NIRs adhere to regional policies, as they are APNIC Members.
-Each economy may only have one NIR. As mentioned on the previous slide, there are seven NIRs in the APNIC region.
-NIRs can have their own policies that apply only to their members, but those policies must not conflict with regional and global policies.

**Topic 1.3 — APNIC Policies**

What is apinic policies?

-APNIC Policies are the rules that control the way IP addresses and Autonomous system numbers are distributed and registered in the Asia Pacific.

Why do we need Policies?
IP addresses and AS numbers are public shared resources.
APNIC policies ensure that these resources are managed properly and distributed with the goal of fairness and consistency in mind.

*APNIC Policies*
Apnic policies are decided and developed by members and internet community in a bottom up process of consultation and consensus. 
-open
-transparent
-bottom up

*Policy Development Process*

Open
Anyone in the community can propose a Policy.
This can be a proposed change to an existing policy or a new one altogether.
Anyone can participate from the beginning, during the discussion as well as in the decision-making process.

Transparent
APNIC publicly documents all policy discussions and decisions to provide complete transparency of the policy development process.
These documents, the associated discussion in the mailing list, and decisions are freely available for viewing at any time.


Bottom-up
Policy Development Process is driven by the community - by those who need and use these resources.
It is catered to address the needs and requirements of the Asia-Pacific Internet community.

APNIC manages its resource delegation, registration and transfers based on three key policies of the APNIC Internet Number Resource Policies document.
IPv4 Policy
IPv6 Policy
ASN Policy
